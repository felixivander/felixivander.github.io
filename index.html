Hey!!
I'm a fourth-year undergraduate at the University of Toronto, studying Chemistry, Physics, and Mathematics. Currently, I am working under Prof. Dvira Segal to study quantum transport at the nanoscale. We will be exploring neighboring topics for my final thesis.
I moved from Indonesia in 2018, initially to read philosophy. In my first year, I found chemistry and physics much more entertaining – I immediately changed discipline.
I have many interests, but now I focus on those in the intersection of theory, experiment, and computation.
In my spare time I enjoy working out, playing football or badminton, and watching movies. I also dabble in reading non-fiction books.
Previously, I worked under Prof. David C. Stone in designing an open-access Gas Chromatography Simulator. I spent 1 year working with Katelyn Dixon, supervised by Prof. Nazir P. Kherani and Prof. Naomi Matsuura, in using SERS-ML to diagnose cardiovascular disease biomarkers in blood. I also worked briefly with Remy Ko to functionalize gold substrates for SERS and to examine different Raman elements.
